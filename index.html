Research Motivation and Direction

I am currently an exchange student at the University of the West. I am interested in interdisciplinary research directions such as brain-computer interfaces for pain and emotion regulation, and I hope to contribute to practical work that supports human well-being.

My undergraduate research focuses on reducing hallucination in large language models through Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). In my completed NSTC undergraduate project on diabetes health education, I designed and built the project database, then transformed complex unstructured data into a unified structured format for reliable retrieval and generation.

At this stage, HCAI, HCI, and BCI are future goals rather than areas where I already have deep specialization. While I do not yet fully understand the biological mechanisms at the neuron level, I have strong data extraction and engineering capabilities. I can help a lab build automated literature retrieval systems, process large experimental metadata, and use AI tools to improve research workflows. I am also interested in psychology; I completed a general psychology course in university and received a score of 90.
